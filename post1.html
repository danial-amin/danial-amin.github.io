Natural Language Processing

Danial Amin
October 31, 2022

The field of computer science and more especially the subfield of AI devoted to teaching computers to interpret written and spoken language in a manner similar to that of humans is known as natural language processing (NLP). Natural language processing (NLP) integrates statistical, machine learning, and deep learning models with computational linguistics (the modelling of human language according to predetermined rules). When combined, these tools provide computers the ability to "understand" the whole meaning of human language, including the speaker's or writer's purpose and emotion, in the form of text or audio data. With the use of natural language processing (NLP), computers can translate across languages, understand spoken commands, and quickly summarise vast amounts of text, sometimes in real time. NLP is likely something you've encountered in the form of voice-operated GPS devices, digital assistants, speech-to-text dictation software, customer support chatbots, and other modern conveniences. However, NLP is also being used in corporate solutions that aim to improve business efficiency, boost staff output, and simplify crucial company procedures.
